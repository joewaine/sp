VIDEO: Joe Waine – “Fabio Kush”/"Owen's Interlude"

Oliver Reinhart // April 1st, 2019
Share < br />this article:
 Share
 Tweet
CREDIT: Koji Minami

Ah, Seattle! Home to legends like Nirvana, Pearl Jam, and Jimi Hendrix. Great music is born out of great places, and the PNW is no exception to that rule. In fact, there’s even a festival, dubbed Wainestock, celebrating all the burgeoning acts across the city. And, no, it’s not a reference to Wayne’s World (though I’ve been known to reference Wayne’s World whenever I can) it’s named after today’s featured musician, Joe Waine.

There’s a certain aura to Joe Waine’s music that’s both uniquely Pacific Northwest, and totally it’s own. Maybe it’s his casual flirtations with a Mellotron; the glisteningly calm undertones of his tunes; or maybe even just his time under the grey skies. Whatever his recipe, over here at SPIN we’re into it, so we were more than excited to hear Waine has ventured into creating music videos as well.

The title track off of his 2020 release, Fat Dreams of Italy, “Fabio Kush” sets the tone for the whole album with whimsical, psych-tinged indie-pop, complete with guitar flourishes and atmospheric textures. “Fabio Kush” feels at once hazy and clear, like waking up with a thought right on the tip of your tongue. Countryish piano, a rough but sensitive baritone, gentle percussion: if only we could get a swirl of flavors like this at our local frozen yogurt shop.

Of course, the dreamy ditty arrives with its own music video, which showcases Joe Waine and friends, adorned in their 17th century finest, frolicking in wind-shimmering lavender fields with -- you guessed it -- miniature horses. There’s swing dancing, heaps of visual ASMR, and an entire masquerade sequence -- all with a harpsichord-laden bonus track. Schwing!

Joe Waine’s “Fabio Kush” is a wonderful example of musician-turned-auteur, so check it out below.
